Overview


HipHerd is a consumer product geared towards millenials and Gen Z so one of the co-founders of HipHerd.com took it seriously when I told him the visual design of their web app was a little...dated. The designs felt old, lifeless, and too much like a SaaS product. He challenged me to change it up to fit today's standards of "modern" design. The changes are outlined in this case study.


Project role


Visual design consultant

The Changes


Onboarding








The original onboarding screens had a gray background with white components which showed its lack of branding. The text fields and buttons were more square, which made it reminiscent of a SaaS product. Overall, the original design seemed too formal, conflicting with the nature of the product.

The improved design removes the gray background, allowing for a minimal and cohesive aesthetic. For the same reason, the text fields are rounder and the buttons are more pill like. In other areas of HipHerd, illustrations from Undraw were being used so I decided to add similar illustrations with blobs behind them to add color to otherwise plain pages. The illustrations help add character and make the site feel more approachable, playful, and fun, which not only better fits a consumer product model but also better suits HipHerd as a brand.
